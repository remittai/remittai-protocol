# REMITTAI — AI-Powered Remittance Token on Stellar

## Executive Summary

### The Problem

The global remittance market moves $800B+ annually, yet the infrastructure is broken:

- **Fees are predatory.** The global average cost to send $200 is 6.2% ($12.40). The UN SDG target is 3%. Western Union and MoneyGram charge 5-10%+ with hidden FX markups on top.
- **Speed is unacceptable.** Bank wires take 3-5 business days. Even "instant" services often hold funds for 24-48 hours.
- **1.4 billion adults are unbanked.** Traditional remittance rails require bank accounts on both ends. The people who depend most on remittances are the least served.
- **FX rates are opaque.** Providers mark up exchange rates by 2-4% on top of stated fees, effectively doubling the real cost.
- **Corridors are underserved.** Africa-to-Africa, Southeast Asia intra-regional, and small Pacific Island corridors have the highest fees (10-15%+) and worst coverage.

### The Solution: RemittAI ($RMAI)

RemittAI is an AI-powered remittance protocol built on Stellar that:

1. **Cuts fees to under 1%** by using Stellar's native DEX and path payments for atomic multi-currency settlement
2. **Settles in 5-7 seconds** with absolute finality (no chargebacks, no holds)
3. **Uses AI to optimize every transfer** — route selection, FX timing, liquidity aggregation, and fraud detection
4. **Serves the unbanked** via mobile money and cash-agent integrations through Stellar anchors
5. **Remains fully compliant** using Stellar's built-in authorization, clawback, and SEP regulatory framework

### The Token: $RMAI

$RMAI is the utility token powering the RemittAI protocol:

- **Fee payment** — All protocol fees are paid in $RMAI (at a discount vs. fiat)
- **Liquidity provision** — Market makers stake $RMAI to provide corridor liquidity and earn yields
- **AI oracle rewards** — AI agents that provide FX optimization and fraud scoring earn $RMAI
- **Governance** — Token holders vote on corridor expansion, fee structures, and protocol upgrades
- **Burn mechanism** — A percentage of protocol fees are used to buy back and burn $RMAI
